TechTogether's First Annual Hacker Awards

Virtual • March 13th, 2021 • 7- 10pm EST

TechTogether's first awards ceremony. A virtual celebration of your success this 2020-2021 hackathon season.

Celebrating Excellence in the TechTogether Community

Our community is the force that drives TechTogether's mission forward. The Annual Hacker Awards are a celebration of all that we have accomplished as a collective this year. Whether you were nominated for an award or attending to support a friend, we hope you will join us for this momentous event in TechTogether's history.
All ticket revenue will be doubled by a donor and go to expanding TechTogether's impact. Award recipients receive a certificate of achievement and an invitation to a private virtual dinner with all food expenses covered by TechTogether.

Awards

Fire-Starter Award

The Firestarter Award will be awarded to a high school hacker that has distinguished themselves through their courage, professional growth, and contributions to the TechTogether community.

Organizer Award

The Oustanding Organizer Award will be awarded to the organizer who has distinguished themselves through their contributions to the TechTogether community and legacy.

Alumni Award

The Alumn Award will be awarded to a TechTogether alumn who has distinguished themselves through their professional accomplishments.

Mentor Award

The Mentor Award is designed to recognize a volunteer that has provided outstanding service to the TechTogether community.

Hacker Award

The Hacker Award will be awarded to the team with the most outstanding TechTogether hackathon project.

Advocate Award

The Advocate Award is designed to recognize a company that has provided outstanding service to the TechTogether community.

Resilience Award

The Resilience Award will be awarded to a hacker from an untraditional background that has distinguished themselves through their courage, professional growth, and contributions to the TechTogether community.

Nominate a community member for an award.

Fireside Chat

Arlan Hamilton

Arlan Hamilton is the Founder and Managing Partner of Backstage Capital, a venture capital firm dedicated to minimizing funding disparities in tech by investing in high-potential founders who are people of color, women, and/or LGBT. She is also co-founder of Backstage Studio, which launched a four-city accelerator program in 2018. Arlan has been featured on the cover of Fast Company magazine as the first Black woman non-celebrity to do so, and is the author of “It’s About Damn Time” and host of the weekly podcast “Your First Million”. Arlan and her mother, Mrs. Earline Butler-Sims, announced their scholarship program which kicked-off with scholarships for Black students at Oxford University and HBCU Dillard University.

Keynote

Megan Smith

Smith is an award-winning entrepreneur, engineer, and tech evangelist. Today Smith is CEO and founder of shift7, a company working collaboratively on systemic social, environmental and economic problems - finding opportunities to scout and scale promising solutions and solution makers and engage proven tech-forward, open, shareable practices to drive direct impact, together. Smith served as the third United States Chief Technology Officer from 2014-2017 helping the President and his teams harness the power of data, innovation and technology on behalf of the nation. Smith recruited top tech talent to serve across government collaborating on pressing issues, from data science, AI and open source, to inclusive economic growth and criminal justice reform. Her teams focused on broad capacity building by co-creating all-hands-on-deck initiatives, including the public-private program TechHire, the Computer Science for All initiative, and the Image of STEM campaigns.

Musical Performance

Chel

Born in Springfield Illinois, singer/songwriter Chel knew she wanted to leave her mark on the world. Singed with International modeling agency Dorothy Combs Modeling. She’s performed at CurvyCon, walked in NYFW, featured in Billboard Magazine, hosts on Dash Radio, and Headlined both Pride Fest and Summer Fest. Chel is a pop force to watch. Growing up her parents joked that she came out singing and later on she attended EI institute where she got her break. She went on to tour with Murphy Lee, Chingy, and David Correy. She has opened up for acts like Nelly and Jason Derulo. Her story is a tale of perseverance, hard work, and overcoming the odds stacked against her. It's a story familiar to many and Chel’s determination along her journey has resonated and inspired them. She has quickly become a prominent influencer with an audience that spans millions across multiple platforms. She uses her growing platform to spread body positivity and the power of individuality.

Event Host

Michelle Kwok

Michelle is Co-Founder and CEO of the Female Laboratory of Innovative Knowledge (FLIK) and built the first female-focused apprenticeship portal, allowing female leaders and apprentices to connect and work together. Having previously worked in traditionally male-dominated industries, such as sports, business and tech, Michelle discovered her niche at the intersection of technology and impact. Michelle leads FLIK to achieve equity and actively cultivates a community of ethnic minorities, diverse socioeconomic statuses and women from isolated communities. The apprenticeship program is available remotely, enabling women in rural communities to gain equal access to opportunities as those living in urban communities. Noticing that females are the subject of less than 25% of news stories, Michelle set out to document untold stories of visionary women and share them on the platform.
